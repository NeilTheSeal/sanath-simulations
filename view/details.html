Details

This Demonstration depicts the flow from a tank where the liquid is maintained at a constant height. It shows that the liquid's flow rate is proportional to the square root of its height. The program calculates the volumetric flow rate, Q (m³/s), using a slightly modified Bernoulli equation:

Q = 2⁢g⁢h2c₀π(d/100)²/4

Where g is the gravitational constant (9.81 m/s²), h is the liquid's height in the tank (m), c₀ is the discharge (orifice) coefficient (dimensionless), and d is the drain diameter (cm). The corresponding mass flow rate (kg/s) is calculated by multiplying the volumetric flow rate, Q, by the liquid's density, ρ (kg/m³).

The discharge coefficient c₀ is related to the resistance coefficient, k, by the following equation:

k = 1/c₀²
